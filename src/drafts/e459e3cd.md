For the last few days my daily setup has been OpenAI plus OpenClaw, running on subscription tokens through the Codex CLI. It works better than it ever has.

A few weeks ago that would have sounded odd. If you were running an agent harness — the orchestration layer that turns raw model calls into agentic workflows — Anthropic was the default. Claude was the best model, and the rest of the stack bent to fit it.

That is not the setup I would recommend to a peer today.

Not because Claude got worse. At the top end, Anthropic may still have the best model. My argument is simpler: **the economics under AI are shifting, and the teams that win the next phase will be the ones who kept their harness vendor-agnostic and their workflows portable.**

Optionality is the strategy right now.

## The subsidized-token era is ending

The last two years were strange. Twenty dollars a month. Unlimited-ish usage. Chat windows absorbing work that clearly cost more to serve. Providers wanted adoption and share, so the real cost of intelligence was hidden under subscriptions and growth logic.

That phase does not last forever.

Tokens stop being a marketing expense and start being margin. The difference between a subscription token and an API token stops being a billing detail and becomes an architectural constraint.

Anthropic has pushed third-party harness usage toward the API. If you want Claude inside OpenClaw or LangGraph, the practical path is pay-per-token, and those tokens have gotten more expensive, not less.

OpenAI has moved the other way. Subscription tokens through the Codex CLI now feed a real agent harness from the same flat-fee plan that gives me a chat window. Add GPT Image 2, and OpenAI becomes hard to ignore on price-performance.

## Anthropic is building the walls

The pricing move is not random. It fits a larger strategy.

Claude Code plus Cowork. Claude Design. Managed Agents in the cloud. Every layer a team might otherwise reach outside Anthropic for is being pulled back inside.

My take: this is Apple's playbook, dressed up for frontier AI.

Apple did not win by having the best individual product in every category. It won by making the whole stack feel inevitable — wired together well enough that leaving feels more expensive than staying.

Anthropic is running the same pattern, faster and cleaner. Build the walls. Wait for organizations to commit. Then find out how much they will pay to stay.

That can work — but only if the model lead remains real.

## The Mythos bet

The competition is closing from multiple directions. OpenAI is re-accelerating. Gemini has gotten quietly competitive. The open-source wave out of China is credible, improving, and running on different economics.

That is why Mythos matters.

Mythos is the rumored next-generation Anthropic model and putative successor to Opus. The name is almost too on-the-nose — a model called *Mythos* trading on its own mythology.

I do not know whether Mythos is a genuine breakthrough or the best-run PR operation in frontier AI this year. But the Apple-playbook move only holds if the flagship keeps its lead. Opus may justify the premium today. Mythos has to justify the *next* premium, against sharper competition and buyers auditing AI spend.

The timing matters too. Ship before IPO, and the IPO is priced against proof. Ship after, and it is priced against anticipation. The choice tells you something about how confident they actually are.

## Optionality is the operating model

I am not telling anyone to abandon Anthropic. Claude is excellent, and for plenty of tasks it is still the right call. If Mythos lands the way the rumors want it to, the premium may justify itself again.

What I am saying is narrower: do not commit to a proprietary harness yet.

Do not re-architect your team around one vendor's managed-agents surface because the demo was good. Do not confuse a beautiful walled garden with a durable operating model.

The future is model selection per workflow. Reasoning-heavy work may justify the most expensive model you can access. High-volume refactoring, classification, extraction, and validation probably should not.

The winning AI operating model will not be "pick the best model." It will be "pick the right model for this workflow, at this cost, with this failure mode."

That is hard to do from inside a walled garden.

So keep your harness vendor-agnostic. Keep your workflows portable. Measure performance per task, not per brand. Preserve the ability to change your mind quickly.

In a market where the top can change in a quarter, optionality is not a hedge. It is the operating model.

The teams that keep it will spend the next phase routing work. The teams that traded it away will spend it explaining invoices.
